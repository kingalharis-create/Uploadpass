Our story

About UploadPass

"I built UploadPass after my own passport photo got rejected three times — not because it looked bad, but because the file was too large. I couldn't find a simple, free tool that just fixed it. So I made one."

Why UploadPass exists

Government and employer portals have strict technical requirements for photo uploads — exact pixel dimensions, file size limits, and specific formats. Most people don't know these requirements exist until their photo gets rejected.

Standard photo apps let you crop and filter, but they don't help you hit an exact file size or pixel count. That gap is what UploadPass fills. Upload any photo, select your application type, and get a compliant file in seconds.

How it works

UploadPass runs entirely in your browser. When you upload a photo, it never leaves your device. There is no server, no database, no account. The tool uses JavaScript to resize, crop, and compress your image locally, then lets you download the result immediately.

This approach means your photo is completely private by design, not just by policy.

Who built this

UploadPass is an independent project built and maintained by King Alharis. It is not affiliated with any government agency, embassy, or employer. Questions or feedback are always welcome at kingalharis@gmail.com.

Free forever

The core tool — fixing a single photo to meet government specs — will always be free. No account, no credit card, no watermark. A Pro version with bulk processing and additional features is available as a one-time optional upgrade.
