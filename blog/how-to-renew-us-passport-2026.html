How to Renew Your US Passport in 2026 — Step by Step

Updated April 2026 · 5 min read

Renewing a US passport is more straightforward than most people expect, but small mistakes — especially with your photo — can delay the process by weeks. This guide walks you through every step so you get it right the first time.

Do You Qualify to Renew by Mail?

Most adults can renew their passport by mail using Form DS-82, which is faster and easier than applying in person. You qualify if all of the following are true:

  • Your most recent passport is available and undamaged
  • It was issued when you were 16 or older
  • It was issued within the last 15 years
  • It was issued in your current legal name, or you can document a name change

If any of these don't apply, you'll need to apply in person using Form DS-11 at a passport acceptance facility.

What You'll Need

  • Completed Form DS-82 (download from travel.state.gov)
  • Your most recent passport
  • One passport photo meeting all US requirements
  • Payment — $130 for a passport book, $30 for a passport card
  • Name change documentation if applicable (certified marriage certificate, court order)

Step 1 — Fill Out Form DS-82

Download and print Form DS-82 from the US State Department website. Fill it out in black ink. Do not sign it until instructed — for mail renewals you sign in front of a notary or just sign and mail it depending on the current year's instructions.

Step 2 — Get Your Passport Photo Right

This is where most renewals run into problems. The US State Department requires your digital or printed passport photo to meet very specific technical standards.

US passport photo requirements

  • Size: exactly 2 × 2 inches (600 × 600 pixels for digital)
  • Format: JPEG
  • File size: under 240 KB
  • Background: plain white
  • Face must be centered, looking directly at the camera
  • No glasses, no hats, neutral expression

Step 3 — Assemble Your Application

Put your application together in this order: completed DS-82 form, passport photo clipped (not stapled) to the form, your most recent passport, any name change documents, and your check or money order made out to "U.S. Department of State."

Step 4 — Mail Your Application

Send everything to the address listed on the DS-82 instructions. Use a trackable mailing method like USPS Priority Mail with tracking. Keep copies of everything before you send it.

How Long Does It Take?

As of 2026, routine processing takes 6 to 8 weeks. Expedited processing (add $60) takes 2 to 3 weeks. If you have travel within 14 days, you can request an urgent appointment at a regional passport agency.

Tracking Your Passport

Once your application is processed, you can track your passport status at travel.state.gov using your last name, date of birth, and the last four digits of your Social Security number.
