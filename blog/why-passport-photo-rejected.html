Why Your Passport Photo Keeps Getting Rejected

Updated April 2026 · 4 min read

Having your passport photo rejected is frustrating — especially when it looks perfectly fine to you. The truth is that most rejections have nothing to do with how the photo looks. They are caused by technical issues that government portals check automatically before a human ever sees your photo.

Here are the most common reasons and exactly how to fix each one.

The Most Common Rejection Reasons

01
File size too large

This is the number one reason for rejection. The US passport portal requires photos under 240 KB. A photo taken on a modern smartphone is typically 3 to 8 MB — more than 10 times too large. The portal rejects it instantly, automatically, before anyone looks at it.

02
Wrong pixel dimensions

US passport photos must be exactly 600 × 600 pixels. A photo that is 598 × 600 or 610 × 610 will be rejected. Most photo apps crop to approximate sizes without hitting the exact pixel count required.

03
Wrong file format

Most government portals accept JPEG only. PNG and HEIC files — which iPhones produce by default — are commonly rejected even if the dimensions and file size are correct.

04
Background not white enough

A background that is light grey, cream, or slightly off-white will sometimes pass and sometimes fail depending on the portal's automated checks. Pure white (#ffffff) is the safest choice.

05
Face too small or too large in frame

Your face must take up between 70% and 80% of the photo frame. If you are standing too far away or too close to the camera, the photo may be rejected by automated face-detection checks.

The pattern: Most of these rejections happen before a human sees your photo. Automated systems check file size, dimensions, and format first. If any of these fail, the application stops there.

Tips for Taking a Better Passport Photo at Home

Lighting

Stand facing a window with natural light. Avoid lamps above your head which cast shadows under your eyes and chin. Even, diffused light gives the cleanest result.

Background

Stand against a plain white wall. If your wall is off-white, move as close to it as possible to reduce shadows. A bedsheet or large piece of white card works well too.

Expression and pose

Look directly at the camera, mouth closed, neutral expression. No glasses. Hair pulled back from the face is recommended so the face detection software can clearly identify your features.
