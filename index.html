Why do passport and visa photos get rejected?

The most common reason passport and visa photos get rejected is a technical specification mismatch — not the quality of the photo itself. Government portals and employment systems like USAJOBS apply strict automated checks for file size, pixel dimensions, and file format. When a photo fails these checks, it is rejected automatically before any human reviews it.

Most standard photo apps do not make it easy to export an image at an exact file size. That is the problem UploadPass solves: upload any photo, select your application type, and the tool automatically resizes and compresses the image to meet the precise requirements.

US Passport photo requirements

The US State Department requires passport photos to be exactly 2×2 inches (600×600 pixels), saved as a JPEG file, under 240KB in file size, with a plain white background. Digital submission rejections are almost always caused by the file being too large or the dimensions being slightly off.

How to fix USAJOBS photo upload errors

USAJOBS requires photos to be at least 100×100 pixels and under 5MB. If you are receiving an upload error on USAJOBS, select the USAJOBS preset, upload your photo, and download the fixed version. The output will meet USAJOBS specifications and upload without errors.

Schengen visa photo requirements

Schengen visa photos must be 35×45mm in color with a white or off-white background. The face must be centered and take up 70–80% of the frame. When submitting digitally, the file must typically be under 240KB in JPEG format.
